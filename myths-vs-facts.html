Myths vs Facts

Unveiling the truth about women’s health, one myth at a time.

Common Myths & Facts

Myth

Periods make a woman impure.

Fact

Periods are a natural process — they do not define purity.

Myth

You shouldn’t bathe during your period.

Fact

Bathing helps relaxation and hygiene during menstruation.

Myth

Exercise should be avoided during periods.

Fact

Light workouts or yoga can ease cramps and help mood swings.

Myth

Women shouldn’t eat sour or spicy food during periods.

Fact

You can eat whatever your body feels comfortable with!

Myth

Tampons or cups break virginity.

Fact

Virginity is a social concept — hygiene products don’t affect it.

Myth

PMS is just in your head.

Fact

PMS is real and affects both mind and body due to hormonal shifts.

Myth

Women’s periods always sync up when they live together.

Fact

There’s no strong evidence that period syncing is biological.

Myth

It’s okay to wear one pad for the whole day.

Fact

Change regularly to maintain hygiene and prevent infections.

Myth

Period pain is exaggerated.

Fact

For some women, cramps are intense — they deserve care, not judgment.

Myth

You can’t swim during your period.

Fact

With proper hygiene, swimming is completely safe during periods.

Myth

Men shouldn’t know or talk about periods.

Fact

Open conversations create awareness and empathy across genders.

Myth

Women should rest completely during their periods.

Fact

Women can do anything — even during their period days!

Breaking the Taboo

Talking openly about menstruation and women’s health helps eliminate stigma and encourages better understanding for all. Empowerment begins with awareness.
